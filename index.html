About

Novice programmer with aspirations to get into the game design or web design community. I use many different programming languages in my work, mainly HTML5, C# and Java but have touched on PHP, SLGL, C and JavaScript. I enjoy photography and 3D modelling as it lets my imagination run free and gets me away from programming to unwind. I try my best to create 3D models that I can use for animation or in games and the photography comes in handy when I want to do some editing or for inspiration.

I aim to gain a much further understanding of Java, HTML5 and C#. With technology advancing ever faster it’s important to keep up to date with new developments and approaches to digital design. I have been recently looking into node.js and microservices which is really advanced and more back-end compared to what I normally do but interesting nonetheless. For front-end I’ve been looking into Vue and React these are both upcoming JavaScript libraries capable of creating better functionality and user interfaces.

I use variety of different software packages when creating my work and have found that it’s made me versatile in my understanding of how they integrate into one another. Some of my favourite programs for creating my work are: Brackets, Unity, AutoDesk’s Maya and of course, the Adobe family. These high end, professional programs are the fundamental building blocks in the design and development of many applications and digital art.

Projects

This is a model I created on Maya, it is a M4A1 carbine rifle and was one of my early model creations this was when I was more into modelling and was probably the most intricate modelling project I have done so far. The model took me roughly 10 hours and is made up of many different parts that can be blown up to see how it’s put together. The model hasn’t been in any games as of yet but I might find a use for it in a future project. I will be taking this project further in the future and would like to create some nice animations of the model falling apart or maybe an animation of how it works. The scope was an interesting piece to make as it was a glass material. I have since had much more experience with such materials and use them inmy modelling quite frequently.


This in my opinion is the best 3D Modelling project I have created this was some work that I created during my second year at university, it captures the essence of a futuristic factory. It was created using Autodesk Maya, during this project I learned many new skills including how to use mash, create electricity and nCloth. The models were created and textured inside Maya using Arnold materials to give me the best looking materials and renders.

I will continue to better my skills as a 3D designer and animator as I continue to study at university. I am currently working on an animation that include a character and storyline, this will be my most challenging project yet as it will need to have realistic motion in the movement of the character.
